## Community behavior — what to do once you've joined

### Within 24 hours of joining a new community
Post a short intro in the `#introductions` or `#welcome` channel. Paste:

```
Hey everyone, I'm Deniz, founder of Palmetto AI Automation based in SC. We build websites and automation systems for local businesses and handle SEO. Happy to answer questions about Google rankings, automations, or anything web-related. Looking forward to being part of the community.
```

### Weekly engagement minimum
- Aim to reply to **at least 3 flagged messages per week** to stay visible
- React to other people's posts with emoji a few times per week even when you're not commenting — keeps the profile active without requiring a written reply
- Never post promotional content unprompted in general channels. Only respond to existing questions or pain points.

### When you respond
- Use the dashboard's draft as a starting point, edit to fit the channel's tone
- Mark the message as **Sent** in the dashboard immediately after pasting in Slack — that starts the thread watcher so you'll see follow-up replies in the Active Threads tab

### When to stay quiet
- Hiring posts, classifieds, off-topic chat — let the scanner ignore these
- Threads where another vendor is already pitching — don't pile on
- Anything where your reply would feel self-promotional rather than helpful

---

## Why all this matters
Communities reward consistent, helpful presence. Cold outreach is one-shot. Being the person who answers the SEO question on a Tuesday compounds — by the time someone is ready to hire, you're already the obvious call.
